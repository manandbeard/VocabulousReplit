System Prompt: Act as an expert presentation designer for a secondary English classroom. Convert the following lesson plan into a highly readable, visually striking slide deck.

Design Rules:

Typography: Use clear, large, dark charcoal sans-serif fonts.

Layout: Keep on-screen text minimal; move detailed instructions to the Speaker Notes.

Aesthetic: All graphics must feature a bold, textured screen print / risograph style (flat colors, halftone patterns, slightly offset registration, vintage/artistic look). No photography.

Background: Faded 70s mustard-yellow with a subtle grain texture.

Slide 1: Welcome & Setup
Visual: A stylized risograph graphic of a vintage hourglass with sand pouring through.

Title: Welcome to Thursday

Action: * Grab your SSR book.

We begin with 10 minutes of peaceful reading.

Speaker Notes: Enforce the quiet reading norm immediately. Sit and read your own book to model the behavior for the students.

Slide 2: SSR & Reading Log
Visual: A simple halftone graphic of a ticking clock.

Title: Log Your Progress

Content:

Time: 10 Minutes.

Pages: Update your tracker.

Quote: One line that caught your eye.

Summary: 1–2 sentences.

Speaker Notes: Give a 2-minute warning before transitioning to the day's activity.

Slide 3: The Cognitive Overload Test
Visual: A bold, textured graphic of a dense block of text with a "Caution" symbol.

Title: The Working Memory Test

Content:

The Task: Read the text on the board.

The Goal: Memorize the core meaning and write a one-sentence summary.

The Rule: You have 2 minutes of silent "Everybody Writes" time.

Speaker Notes: Project a dense paragraph (e.g., the U.S. Constitution or a Shakespearean sonnet). Teacher Move: The moment they start reading, play a loud, repetitive commercial jingle on your speakers.

Slide 4: The Debrief: Why was that hard?
Visual: A risograph graphic of a human head with "static" or zigzag lines coming out of it.

Title: Sieve vs. Sand

Content:

Why was that nearly impossible?

What did the noise do to your brain?

Connection: Constant sensory input stops the brain from holding onto complex thought.

Speaker Notes: Explain the metaphor: The Sieve is the mind; the Sand is the knowledge falling through the holes.

Slide 5: Reading Focus: The Subway Ride
Visual: A halftone silhouette of a speeding subway train.

Title: Part 2: The Sieve and the Sand

Content:

Focus: Montag is fighting to build his agency.

Watch for: The moment Montag tries to read the Bible on a train.

Instruction: Listen for the commercial: "Denham's Denham's Denham's...".

Speaker Notes: Have students follow along as you read through the section where Montag encounters the radio commercial.

Slide 6: Discussion: The Weapon of Distraction
Visual: A graphic of a "Loaded Gun" made out of radio waves or speakers.

Title: Denham’s vs. The Bible

Content:

How is this toothpaste commercial a literal weapon against Montag?

How does this prove Beatty’s claim that speed "flings off" all unnecessary thought?

Action: Turn & Talk (2 Minutes) with your partner.

Speaker Notes: Encourage students to rehearse their answers before the whole-class discussion to boost their SL11 (Speaking & Listening) performance.

Slide 7: The Text-Tether (SL11 Challenge)
Header: Find the Evidence

The Search: Find the exact sentence on page 75 (approx.) where the music physically attacks the passengers.

Target Evidence: "The train radio vomited upon Montag... The people were pounded into submission".

Speaker Notes: Validate student answers that connect noise to control, then push them to find the specific "vomited" or "pounded" imagery.

Slide 8: Exit Ticket: Your Agency
Visual: A bold risograph graphic of a single key or a door opening.

Title: Reflection

Content: * What is the "Denham’s Dentifrice" in your own life?

What noise stops you from having deep thoughts?

Speaker Notes: This is a metacognitive check to connect Bradbury’s 1950s warning to their 2026 reality.
